Metea Valley SkillsUSA


About SkillsUSA


SkillsUSA is the #1 workforce development organization for students, serving more than 395,000 middle school, high school, and college students as well as professional members enrolled in training programs for trade, technical and skilled service occupations. The organization empowers students to become skilled professionals, career-ready leaders, and responsible community members.



Our Goal


SkillsUSA is a national organization focused on empowering students to become world-class workers and leaders in their fields. The goal of our SkillsUSA club is to provide students in career and technical education (CTE) programs with opportunities to develop leadership skills, technical expertise, and a strong work ethic. We help students build the skills necessary to succeed in their future careers by promoting technical training, professionalism, and hands-on experiences. The organization connects students with industry professionals and mentors to enhance their learning. Through a variety of leadership training programs, competitions, and opportunities to take on active roles within the organization, Metea's SkillsUSA encourages students to build leadership skills, teamwork, and confidence. The organization also organizes competitions at local, state, and national levels where students can showcase their skills in areas like welding, construction, health sciences, business, IT, and more. These competitions challenge students and reward excellence in their chosen fields. SkillsUSA also fosters industry partnerships, aligning educational programs with real-world job requirements to ensure that students are trained in the skills employers need most. In addition, the organization encourages students to give back to their communities through service projects, helping them develop a sense of responsibility and civic engagement. Ultimately, we aim to prepare students for successful careers by helping them develop both the technical skills and the personal qualities necessary to thrive in the workforce.
